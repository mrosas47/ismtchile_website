Presentation

The R package ismtchile:: was conceived with the objective of facilitating the calculation and distribution of the Socio-Material Territorial Index (ISMT). This index was developed by the OCUC from variables derived from questions included in the 2017 Chilean Population Census: dwelling materials, home head scolarity, overcrowding y allegamiento. The full methodology is available here, and is mostly intact, except for the update on the AIM cuts for socio-economic groups to their 2021 version, and the inclusion of rural areas. The indicators described above are derived from variables of the 2017 Census; however, for distribution and usability effects, the process was replicated for the 1982, 1992, 2002 and 2012 Census through the literalization and simultaneous homologations of the variables within each database. This last characteristic is still under development and will be available from version 2.2.x.

Origin

The idea of developing this package originates from the need to count with the ISMT data, either as data or as a shapefile, quickly and without the obligation of searching, either locally or online. Aditionally, a lack of clarity with respect to which information already exists, its location, or last update. The package solves these needs by establishing a linear and easy-to-follow workflow, making available both the original census data, necessary for the calculations, and the shapefiles for result spacialization. It is important, however, to note that the development of the package encountered a number of limitations.
